Binary System Evolution

Introduction:
Most massive stars in the Universe are in binary systems, orbiting comparable mass stars. These binaries are responsible for forming many of the most interesting astrophysical phenomena in the Universe, including x-ray binaries, gamma-ray bursts, millisecond pulsars, luminous red novae and merging compact object binaries, observable with gravitational waves.
COMPAS includes a rapid binary population synthesis code, capable of evolving binary systems in a fraction of a second. COMPAS was initially developed to study the merging compact binaries now regularly being observed by the LIGO (www.ligo.caltech.edu) and Virgo (https://www.virgo-gw.eu/) gravitational-wave observatories.
Binary evolution is complicated, and involves many poorly understood astrophysical processes such as massive stellar evolution, mass transfer and common envelope evolution and supernovae. Population synthesis codes like COMPAS parameterise these pieces of physics with simple models fit to more detailed simulations, which can be updated as we gain greater understanding of these processes.
